Discover The Beautiful World

Jammu & Kashmir

Heaven on Earth Kashmir is one of the most beautiful travel destinations to visit in North India. Nowhere in India you will behold the scenic landscapes, icy glaciers, pristine lakes and lofty mountains as beautiful as Kashmir. Serenity and Tranquility redefines itself from the ambiance of Kashmir. Be it summer, Winter or Monsoon, every season has its own charm in Kashmir. The numerous wonders of nature, culture, cuisines and rich history of Kashmir will elevate your travel experience in Incredible India.

Himachal Pradesh

Kullu Manali is a picturesque region in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh, known for its stunning natural beauty. Nestled in the Himalayan mountains, it offers lush green valleys, snow-capped peaks, and serene rivers. Manali is a popular destination for adventure sports like paragliding, skiing, and trekking. The region is also rich in cultural heritage, with temples, traditional villages, and vibrant festivals. Kullu Manali's pleasant climate and breathtaking landscapes make it a sought-after destination for nature lovers and tourists alike.

Leh-Ladakh

Leh Ladakh, located in the northernmost region of India, is renowned for its stark and breathtaking landscapes, characterized by rugged mountains, vast deserts, and crystal-clear lakes. The region is a haven for adventure enthusiasts, offering activities like trekking, mountain biking, and river rafting. Ladakh is also home to ancient Buddhist monasteries, colorful festivals, and a unique blend of Tibetan and Indian culture. The high-altitude desert climate adds to its allure, with dramatic variations in temperature and scenery. Leh, the largest town in the region, serves as a gateway to this remote and mesmerizing part of the Himalayas.

Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand, often referred to as the "Land of the Gods," is a northern Indian state nestled in the Himalayas. Known for its stunning natural beauty, the region boasts snow-capped peaks, lush valleys, serene rivers, and dense forests. Popular destinations include the pilgrimage sites of Haridwar and Rishikesh, the hill stations of Nainital and Mussoorie, and the trekking trails in the Garhwal and Kumaon regions. Uttarakhand is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and spiritual seekers alike, offering a perfect blend of scenic landscapes and cultural heritage.

Amritsar

Amritsar, located in the Indian state of Punjab, is known for its rich history and vibrant culture. The Golden Temple, or Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest Gurdwara of Sikhism, attracting millions of visitors annually. Its stunning architecture, adorned with gold leaf, reflects spiritual significance and beauty. The temple is surrounded by a serene pond, symbolizing purity and tranquility. Amritsar is also famous for its delicious Punjabi cuisine and historical sites, making it a cultural hub.

Goa

Goa, located on the western coast of India, is renowned for its stunning beaches and vibrant nightlife. It has a unique blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures, evident in its architecture and cuisine. The state is famous for its water sports, beach parties, and laid-back atmosphere. Goa also hosts numerous festivals, showcasing its rich traditions and lively spirit. With lush greenery and picturesque landscapes, it’s a popular destination for both relaxation and adventure.

Andaman & Nicobar Islands

The Andaman & Nicobar Islands, located in the Bay of Bengal, are a group of picturesque islands known for their pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life. The islands offer a perfect getaway for those seeking tranquility and natural beauty. The coral reefs surrounding the islands are ideal for snorkeling and scuba diving, while the lush forests are home to diverse flora and fauna. The islands also have a rich history, with remnants of British colonial rule, including the infamous Cellular Jail. The Andaman & Nicobar Islands are a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Rajasthan

Rajasthan, the largest state in India, is known for its majestic forts, palaces, and vibrant culture. Jaipur, the capital city, is often called the "Pink City" due to its unique terracotta-colored buildings. It boasts stunning attractions like the Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and Amber Fort, reflecting its royal heritage. The city is also famous for its bustling markets, where visitors can find traditional handicrafts and textiles. Rajasthan's rich history and colorful festivals make it a captivating destination for travelers.
